About the
Metropolitan League

New York City's first competitive inter-borough pickleball league — bringing the city's independent clubs under one banner.

What it is

One city. One league.

The Metropolitan League is a single, professionally-run competition that connects New York's best independent pickleball clubs — turning casual club play into real, watchable, borough-vs-borough rivalry.

Founded in 2026 and operated by Dink Dynamics, the league exists for a simple reason: the community and competitive energy of NYC pickleball deserves a real stage. Where the big-box clubs offer courts, the Metropolitan League offers a season — programming, coaching, standings, and a reason to show up every week and represent your turf.

Year one is inter-borough. Year two and beyond, the league expands across the Tri-State region.

Why now

The sport is winning

Pickleball is the #1 fastest-growing sport in America three years running — and New York is its most underbuilt major market. Players want a home that's competitive and community-driven. The Metropolitan League is that home.

How it works

A real season

Clubs field squads across three skill divisions — JV, Varsity, and Pro — and meet in club-vs-club fixtures over a tight, committable season of Friday nights. Every match is worth points; every result moves the table.

Community

From the schools to the league

The league is built to give back. Through Pickleball Stars, we run free youth programming in NYC schools; through Sunday Afternoons, we open the courts to the next generation with free play and multi-year mentorship. School nights feed each borough's club — a real pipeline from the playground to the pro tier.
